ExpressVPN Not Working in China? Here is the 2026 Fix

Published: 2026-06-11 | By: Expat Tech Team

If you have recently arrived in China or have been living here for a while, you have likely encountered the dreaded "Connection Failed" screen on ExpressVPN. As of 2026, the Great Firewall (GFW) of China has received massive AI-driven upgrades, making traditional Western VPNs incredibly unreliable.

Why is ExpressVPN Blocked?

ExpressVPN, NordVPN, and Surfshark rely on standard VPN protocols like OpenVPN, IKEv2, and Lightway. While these are excellent for privacy in Western countries, they have highly recognizable digital signatures. The GFW uses Deep Packet Inspection (DPI) to instantly detect these protocols and sever the connection.

What do Locals Use Instead? (The "Airport" Secret)

If traditional VPNs are dead, how are millions of Chinese tech workers and expats accessing Netflix, YouTube, and ChatGPT? The answer lies in Proxy Providers (locally referred to as "Airports").

Instead of routing your traffic through a standard VPN server, these providers use cutting-edge protocols like V2Ray and Trojan. These protocols disguise your encrypted traffic as normal HTTPS web browsing (like visiting Amazon.com). The firewall cannot distinguish it from regular traffic, so it never gets blocked.

The IPLC Advantage

The best proxy providers don't just use disguised protocols; they use IPLC (International Private Leased Circuit) lines. This means your data travels through a dedicated, physical fiber-optic cable from China directly to Hong Kong or Japan, completely bypassing the public firewall. The result is zero packet loss and latency so low you can play foreign games competitively.
